Letterhead Setup Guide

Three things Letterhead needs
to write like you.

This page walks you through what each piece does and why it matters. It takes about five minutes.

01

Add your API key

Letterhead uses an AI model to read job postings and write cover letters. To do that, it needs direct access to the model's API — which means you bring your own key.

Your key lives only in your browser's local storage. It is never sent anywhere except directly to the AI provider's servers, and only when you trigger a generation.

Where to get a key

  1. Go to your AI provider's developer console (e.g. console.anthropic.com).
  2. Create a new API key — name it anything, e.g. letterhead.
  3. Copy it (it starts with sk-ant-…).
  4. Paste it into the API key field on the settings page.

⚠ Keys have usage costs. Most cover letters cost less than $0.01 each.

02

Upload your résumé

Your résumé is the factual backbone. Letterhead reads it to understand your work history, titles, skills, and dates — so the generated letters stay grounded in real things you've done.

Upload a .pdf — Letterhead extracts the text automatically. A cleanly formatted, single-column PDF works best; scanned images won't parse well.

The extracted text is stored locally and never leaves your device except as part of an AI prompt.

03

What is "context"?

An AI model only knows what you tell it. "Context" is the personal briefing you give the model before it writes anything — your voice, your values, what you're looking for, and things your résumé doesn't capture.

Think of it as the conversation you'd have with a ghostwriter over coffee before they write your letter. The richer the context, the more the output sounds like you.

Good things to include

  • Roles or industries you're targeting (and ones you're avoiding)
  • What you're most proud of that doesn't show on a résumé
  • Preferred writing tone — direct and punchy vs. warm and narrative
  • Things you want to emphasize or de-emphasize
  • Why you're looking right now

Example context file

Save this as a context.md file and upload it: 

# About Me

    I'm a product-minded engineer with 6 years of experience across early-stage
    startups and mid-size SaaS companies. I tend to work closest to the customer —
    I've led features end-to-end, written RFCs, done my own user interviews.

    # What I'm Looking For

    Roles at Series A–C companies where engineering and product actually talk.
    Ideally founding-team energy without the 80-hour weeks. I care more about
    autonomy and craft than company name or prestige.

    I'm *not* interested in: big tech, defense, pure infrastructure roles,
    or anything with "10x" in the job description.

    # My Tone

    I write like I talk — direct, no fluff. I'd rather a cover letter sound
    like a real email than a brochure. Skip the "I am excited to apply for…"
    opener. Get to the point.

    # Things the Résumé Doesn't Show

    - I rebuilt our onboarding flow from scratch after watching 40 user sessions
    in a week. Conversion went from 34% to 61%.
    - I'm the person who actually reads the Figma files, not just the tickets.
    - I've mentored two junior engineers who both got promoted within a year.

    # Why I'm Looking

    My current company pivoted to enterprise and I'm more energized by
    consumer-facing products. Looking to make a move in the next 2–3 months.

Plain prose, bullet points, section headers — any structure works. You can always update this file and re-upload as you refine it.
